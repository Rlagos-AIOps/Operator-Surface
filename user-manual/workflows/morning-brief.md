# Your morning brief

The first five minutes of your day.

## Why this matters

The platform's job in your first five minutes is to compress everything that happened overnight into "here's where to spend your attention today." Done right, you sit down, look at three screens, and know exactly what to do next.

The agents have been working overnight. This workflow is how you catch up without reading through every decision they made.

## When you're here

First sign-in of the day. You want to know: what changed while I was away? What needs me?

## The three-minute version

1. **Daily Brief** — read the headline.
2. **Book** — scan for color.
3. **Operator** — pick one item.

Three screens, three minutes. The five-minute version below adds context.

## The five-minute version

### 1. Open the Daily Brief

Click **Daily Brief** in the top nav. (URL: `/brief`.)

**Why this screen first:** The Brief is your team's overnight write-up — what they found while you were away, in their own words. It's the only surface that *synthesizes*; everything else is detail. Start here so the rest of the morning has a shape.

**What to look for:**
- The **headline** — one sentence. What's the most important thing today?
- The **KPI strip** — how big is the queue, how many accounts at-risk, how many renewals within 90 days.
- The **priorities list** — which accounts to look at, in what order, with the agent's reason for each.

You're not reading this carefully. You're reading it like a newspaper front page — get the orientation, move on.

### 2. Open the Book

Click **Book** in the top nav. (URL: `/accounts`.)

**Why this screen next:** The Brief was the team's *opinion*. The Book is your *portfolio in front of you*. Use the Brief to know what to look for; use the Book to scan it yourself in 30 seconds.

**What to look for:**
- The summary line at the top — total ARR, attention count, pending approvals count.
- Cards at the top of the grid — anything 🔴 red or 🟠 orange needs eyes today.
- Cards whose verdicts changed since yesterday — e.g., Lighthouse moving from `at_risk` to `recovering`.
- **Galileo's read** on the top-3 cards — your front-door agent's take in his own voice.

The Book is sorted by attention needed, so the cards that matter most are at the top by default. Trust the order.

### 3. Open the Operator console

Click **Operator** in the top nav. (URL: `/operator`.)

**Why this screen:** The Brief and the Book told you *what* needs attention. The Operator console is *where you act* — the queue, the drafted action, Galileo at your side.

**What to do:**
1. Scan the queue (left panel). Pick the most urgent item — usually the top one.
2. Read the agent's rationale (middle, top).
3. Read the proposed action (middle, composer).
4. If something feels off, ask Galileo (right panel): *"What am I missing about this account?"*
5. Approve, reject, or rewrite.

You don't have to clear the queue today. You have to make **one good decision** and move on. Tomorrow you'll do the same thing again.

## What just happened

In five minutes, you went from "no idea what changed overnight" to "I just made a real decision for a customer." That's the loop. Tomorrow, repeat.

## Tips

- **Resist the urge to read every decision.** The team logs more than you can process; that's the point. Trust the synthesis.
- **One decision is enough.** Triage all morning if you have time, but a single approved action is a successful morning.
- **Ask Galileo for a daily summary.** From `/galileo` (no account selected), try: *"Give me the one-line read on the book today."* — a different way to compress the morning if you prefer chat to the Brief screen.
