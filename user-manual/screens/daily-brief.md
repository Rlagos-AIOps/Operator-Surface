# Daily Brief — `/brief`

The team's overnight write-up, in their own words.

## Why this screen exists

The platform produces more decisions than you can read in a day. The Brief is the only surface that *synthesizes* — it pulls the night's activity into a one-page write-up so you don't have to.

Start your day here. You'll know more in 60 seconds than you would by scrolling Decisions for ten minutes.

## What's on screen

### Header
- A date (today's brief)
- An **AGENT LIVE** strip — when an agent is running right now, the strip pulses volt yellow. (Mostly visible in demos and during scheduled audits.)

### Headline
A single sentence at the top. The most important thing today, as the team sees it. Read this first.

### KPI row
A few count chips: pending approvals, decisions logged, accounts at-risk, renewals within 90 days. Numbers don't summarize the day, but they orient you.

### Body
A short narrative — usually paragraphs and a priority list. The team writes about specific accounts: which to look at first, why, and what they recommend. Real names, real numbers, real reasoning.

### Footer
A timestamp and any "viewed" marker so you know whether the brief is fresh.

## What you can do here

| Action | How |
|---|---|
| **Read the day's read** | Scroll the body. |
| **Drill into a mentioned account** | Use the Book or Decisions to look it up. |
| **Mark as viewed** | Visiting the page is the only signal needed. |

The Brief is intentionally **read-only**. You don't decide on the Brief; you decide on the Operator console.

## When to use this screen

- **First thing in the morning.** The number-one entry point to the day.
- **Returning after a long break** (vacation, weekend) — quickest way to catch up.
- **When you want the team's *opinion*** rather than the underlying data.

## How to read it

- Read the headline. Take 5 seconds with it.
- Scan the KPI row for any number that jumped from yesterday.
- Read the priorities — the team's ordered list of where your attention belongs.
- Skip everything else if you're rushed.

## Tips

- **Don't try to memorize the brief.** It's a synthesis; the source data lives in Decisions and the Book. The brief tells you where to look.
- **The brief is the team's voice, not the platform's.** Treat it like a senior colleague's morning email — opinionated, abbreviated, useful.
- **Briefs are persisted.** Yesterday's brief still exists; if you missed a day, the team wrote one for it.

## What this screen doesn't show

- **The full reasoning behind every claim.** That lives in the Decision Trace (`/decisions`).
- **The actual approvals queue.** That's the Operator console (`/operator`) or Approval Queue (`/approvals`).
- **Per-account portfolio data.** That's the Book (`/accounts`).
