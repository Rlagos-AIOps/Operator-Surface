**SECTION 1 — OVERVIEW**

| **Trigger** | A customer confirms they will not renew or has formally churned. Renewal Opportunity is marked Closed Lost in SFDC. |
| --- | --- |
| **Goal** | Ensure every churn is documented accurately in SFDC, a Churn Retrospective is completed within 24 hours, and the data feeds leadership's understanding of churn patterns so we can prevent the next one. |
| **Why It Matters** | Churn data is only useful if it is honest and specific. Vague Loss Reasons and empty Retrospectives are worse than no data — they create false confidence. Every churn is an investment in preventing the next one. |
| **Target Metrics** | GRR target: 90% (current average: 80%). NRR target: 120% (current average: 101%). Every prevented churn moves these numbers. |
| **Escalation Path** | CSM → Anna → Sarah |
| **Key Rule** | All notes in Chatter (SFDC) — NOT Slack |

**SECTION 3 — SFDC CLOSED LOST FIELD REFERENCE**

**⚠** All fields below are required. Loss Reason and Loss Details are the two most important — be accurate and specific. Leadership tracks these for pattern analysis.

| **SFDC Field** | **How to Complete** | **Why It Matters** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Stage = Closed Lost** | Select Closed Lost from the opportunity stage picklist. | *Triggers the Closed Lost flow and makes the opportunity visible in churn reporting.* |
| **Loss Reason (required)** | Select the most accurate reason from the picklist: Acquisition / EMR Integration Issues / Failure to Implement or Go Live / Integration Features / Lack of Adoption / Loss of Champion or Turnover / Missing Crucial Features / No Decision / Non-Responsive / Not User Friendly / Not Worth It / Other / Out of Business / Product Bugs or Issues / Stopped Paying - No Contact / Too Expensive / Unhappy with Support / Unknown / Merged with another account / Unqualified | *This is the primary churn data point that leadership tracks. Be accurate — not just convenient. "Other" and "Unknown" should be last resorts.* |
| **Loss Details (required)** | Free text. Write a specific, honest summary of what happened. Include: what the customer said, what was tried, when the churn signals first appeared, and what you believe was the root cause. | *This is the record leadership will read. A vague Loss Details field is useless for pattern analysis and improvement.* |
| **Lost to Competitor?** | Yes or No. Be honest. | *Competitive intelligence is critical for product and sales strategy.* |
| **Competitor Lost to** | If Yes — name the competitor. If Unknown, say Unknown. | *Helps identify which competitors are winning and why.* |

**SECTION 4 — CHURN RETROSPECTIVE QUESTION GUIDE**

**ℹ** Complete the Google Form at: <https://docs.google.com/forms/d/1VJthMq02j6N8YisM_BiMqSdrgllsBbFkzxFPUNLpFc0/edit>

Use the guidance below for each question. Vague answers are not useful. Write as if you are briefing Sarah and Marcel on what happened and what we should change.

| **#** | **Question** | **What a Good Answer Looks Like** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1** | **What is the name of the account?** | *Full account name as it appears in SFDC.* |
| **2** | **Customer Success Manager** | *Your name.* |
| **3** | **Did the customer feel confident in their setup coming out of implementation?** | *Be honest. If no, what was missing? Was it a training gap, configuration issue, or expectation mismatch set during sales?* |
| **4** | **What do you think ultimately led this customer to churn?** | *This is the most important question. Do not default to the SFDC Loss Reason — go deeper. What was the real root cause?* |
| **5** | **Do you feel the correct expectations were set during the sales process?** | *If no, be specific about what was overpromised or undercommunicated. This is not about blame — it is about fixing the process.* |
| **6** | **How willing was the customer to work with you on improving their system?** | *Low willingness is itself a signal — was it leadership alignment, change management, or a Lost Champion issue?* |
| **7** | **What are some core features the customer failed to adopt?** | *List specific features. This helps product and CS identify training gaps and missing feature needs.* |
| **8** | **How was this customer's overall adoption?** | *Reference the Product Usage Score if available. Was adoption low from the start, or did it decline over time?* |
| **9** | **Were there any core use cases we couldn't accommodate?** | *Be specific. This is direct product feedback that can prevent future churn.* |
| **10** | **How did pricing factor into their decision to churn?** | *Was pricing the stated reason or the real reason? Sometimes, pricing is the easy answer for a customer who just doesn't see the value.* |
| **11** | **What churn signals did we miss?** | *Be honest with yourself. With hindsight, what would have triggered a Risk Flag or a Churn Save playbook earlier?* |
| **12** | **When did the customer first start questioning if [Company] was the right solution?** | *Early signals matter most. If you can identify when the doubt started, we can work backward to prevent it next time.* |
| **13** | **What could we change so that churn like this doesn't happen again?** | *This is your recommendation to leadership. Be specific and constructive — not vague. "Better onboarding" is not an answer. "Customers who came through Alleva need an extended HyperCare phase" is.* |

**SECTION 5 — KNOWN CHURN PATTERNS (FOR CONTEXT)**

**ℹ** These are the historically identified churn reasons from our book. Use them as a reference when completing the Loss Reason field and Retrospective — if your churn fits a known pattern, call it out explicitly.

| **Churn Reason** | **% of Churn (Historical - source is year 2025)** | **What to Watch For** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Alleva Partnership** | **41%** | Accounts that came through Alleva. Higher churn risk. Watch for customers who feel they were required to buy [Company] without full discovery. Ensure HyperCare and adoption milestones are hit early. |
| **Written Off / Non-Payment** | **28.2%** | Customer stopped paying after cancellation request was declined. Monthly billing accounts are highest risk. Prioritize annual billing conversion at renewal. Follow Mid-Contract Cancellation SOP immediately when payment stops. |
| **Lost to Competitor** | **5.1%** | Document the competitor. Helps product and sales understand competitive gaps. |
| **Went Dark / Non-Responsive** | **5.1%** | Customer stopped engaging entirely. This is a failure of the CSM engagement model. Should have been a Potential Churn Risk flag well before Closed Lost. |
| **Y1 Integration Issues** | **5.1%** | Integration problems in year 1 that were not resolved. Watch for EMR integration issues in HyperCare and Driving Adoption phases. |
| **Acquisition** | **5.1%** | Customer was acquired. Limited prevention ability — but multi-threading and Leadership Alignment playbook can help retain through M&A. |
| **Out of Business** | **2.6%** | Customer closed operations. Monitor Financial Score for early signals. |
| **Leadership Changes** | **2.6%** | New leadership brought their own technology. Lost Champion and Leadership Turnover playbooks exist to address this. |
