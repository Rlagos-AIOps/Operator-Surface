**SECTION 1 — OVERVIEW**

| **Trigger** | The customer's primary internal advocate — the person who championed [Company] internally, drove adoption, and protected the relationship — has left the company or changed roles. |
| --- | --- |
| **Goal** | Identify the new champion quickly, onboard them before they question the platform, and re-establish the multi-threaded relationships that protect the account from a single point of failure. |
| **Why It Matters** | A Lost Champion is one of the most common precursors to churn. The new person does not have the history, the buy-in, or the investment that the previous champion had. If you do not move fast, you are selling [Company] all over again to someone who may have a preferred alternative. |
| **Escalation Path** | CSM → Anna → Sarah |
| **Key Rule** | All notes in Chatter — not Slack. File Lost Champion risk flag immediately. |

**SECTION 2 — PROCESS STEPS**

**⚠** File the Lost Champion risk flag immediately. Do not wait to confirm the departure — file it the moment you learn about it.

| **#** | **Step** | **Action** | **Owner** | **Timing / Resources** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **1** | **File Lost Champion Risk Flag** | Create a Risk Flag record immediately.Risk Type = Lost ChampionStatus = ActiveLog full context in Chatter: who left, when, what their role was, how you found out, and what your initial read on the risk is. | CSM | Same day you learn about itSFDC → Risk\_Flag\_\_c → New RecordRisk Flag SOP (2.3) |
| **2** | **Identify the New Champion Candidate** | Work quickly to identify who will fill the champion role.Ask yourself:- Who did the previous champion report to?- Who is stepping into their role?- Who else on the account has been an engaged user?- Is there an executive who can endorse the platform to the new person?Log your findings in Chatter. Do not wait for the customer to introduce you — be proactive. | CSM | Within 48 hoursSFDC: Account contactsChatter — log research |
| **3** | **Escalate to Anna if No Clear Successor** | If there is no obvious internal successor and the account has no multi-threaded relationships, escalate to Anna immediately.This is a high-risk scenario — a single point of failure with no replacement is a churn signal. | CSM → Anna | If no successor identified within 48 hoursChatter → @Anna |
| **4** | **Reach Out to New Contact** | Send the New Stakeholder Welcome email (Template 3.11) to the new contact.Personalize it — reference the previous champion by name if appropriate, acknowledge the transition, and make clear that you are here to support them.Schedule an onboarding call within the week. | CSM | Within 72 hours of identifying new contactNew Stakeholder Welcome Template (3.11)Chatter — log send |
| **5** | **Run New Stakeholder Onboarding** | Treat this like a mini re-onboarding. See the New Stakeholder Onboarding Playbook (1.12) for the full process.Key goals:- Understand the new stakeholder's priorities and concerns- Demonstrate value with their specific data- Set expectations for the ongoing relationship- Confirm they are aware of and bought into [Company] | CSM | Within 1 week of initial outreachNew Stakeholder Onboarding Playbook (1.12) |
| **6** | **Re-establish Multi-Threading** | Use this transition as an opportunity to add more contacts to the account — not just replace the one who left.Target minimum 2-3 stakeholders: Director of Admissions, Director of BD, and an executive sponsor.Update all contacts in SFDC. Confirm Economic Buyer field is accurate. | CSM | During and after onboardingSFDC → Account → ContactsAccount → Economic Buyer field |
| **7** | **Monitor and Update Risk Flag** | Monitor the new stakeholder's engagement closely for 60 days.Move Risk Flag to Recovering when the new contact has attended at least one call and is actively engaging.Move to Resolved when they are fully onboarded, using the platform, and the relationship is stable. | CSM | Ongoing — 60 day windowRisk\_Flag\_\_c → Status updateChatter — log every touchpoint |
