**SECTION 1 — OVERVIEW**

| **Trigger** | A new stakeholder joins the customer organization in a role that is relevant to [Company] — new hire, promotion, or role change — and needs to be onboarded to the platform and the relationship. |
| --- | --- |
| **Three Scenarios** | 1. Replacement for a lost champion or departed stakeholder (see also Champion Loss Playbook 1.9)2. New hire added to an existing team that already uses [Company]3. Leadership change that brings a new decision maker (see also Leadership Turnover Playbook 1.10) |
| **Goal** | Get the new stakeholder up to speed on [Company] quickly, make them feel supported, connect them to the value their organization has already realized, and establish a direct relationship before they form their own opinion without context. |
| **Escalation Path** | CSM → Anna → Sarah |
| **Key Rule** | All notes in Chatter — not Slack. Update SFDC contacts immediately. |

**SECTION 2 — PROCESS STEPS**

**ℹ** Every new stakeholder is a relationship that needs to be built from scratch. Do not assume they have been briefed by their predecessor or their team. Meet them where they are.

| **#** | **Step** | **Action** | **Owner** | **Timing / Resources** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **1** | **Identify and Add to SFDC** | As soon as you learn of a new stakeholder, add them as a Contact in SFDC and link them to the Account.Capture: name, title, email, phone, and their relationship to [Company] (admin, champion, executive sponsor, etc.).Update the Economic Buyer field if this person is a decision maker. | CSM | Same daySFDC → Account → New ContactAccount → Economic Buyer field (if applicable) |
| **2** | **Send New Stakeholder Welcome Email** | Send the New Stakeholder Welcome email (Template 3.11) within 24 hours.Personalize it — acknowledge the transition, reference what their organization has accomplished with [Company], and invite them to a brief introduction call. | CSM | Within 24 hours of identificationNew Stakeholder Welcome Template (3.11)Chatter — log send |
| **3** | **Schedule Introduction Call** | Request a 30-minute introduction call.Agenda:- Introduce yourself and the CSM relationship- Brief overview of how their organization uses [Company] today- Understand their role and priorities- Set expectations for ongoing engagement- Answer any questions | CSM | Within 1 weekCalendar inviteChatter — log scheduled call |
| **4** | **Run the Introduction Call** | Lead with their data — not a product tour.Show them: what their team has accomplished, what is working well, and what the roadmap looks like.Ask: what are their priorities? What does success look like for them? What would make [Company] more valuable for their role specifically?Log everything in Chatter immediately after. | CSM | Introduction callSFDC: Account Health OverviewProduct usage highlightsChatter — log call notes |
| **5** | **Provide Tailored Resources** | Based on the introduction call, send targeted resources:- If admin/power user: KB articles, Loom training videos, relevant workflow documentation- If director/manager: ROI summary, dashboard overview, QBR format- If executive: Value story with data, forward-looking roadmapDo not send everything at once. One or two relevant resources is more effective than a resource dump. | CSM | Within 24 hours of introduction call[Company] Knowledge BaseLoom training videosHealth Score Dashboard |
| **6** | **Add to Regular Cadence** | Add the new stakeholder to the regular meeting cadence and email communications.Update Meeting Cadence field if their addition changes the engagement frequency.Update Customer Sentiment after the introduction call. | CSM | After introduction callAccount → Meeting\_Cadence\_\_cAccount → Customer\_Sentiment\_\_cChatter — log cadence update |
