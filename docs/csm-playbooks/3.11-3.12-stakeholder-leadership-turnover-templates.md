**TEMPLATE 3.11 — NEW STAKEHOLDER WELCOME**

**ℹ** Sent within 24 hours of identifying a new stakeholder. Warm, brief, and value-led. Makes them feel welcomed into the [Company] relationship before they have a chance to form their own uninformed opinion.

| **When to Send** | Within 24 hours of identifying a new stakeholder — whether a replacement champion, new hire, or newly relevant contact. |
| --- | --- |
| **Sent By** | CSM |
| **Tone** | Warm, welcoming, and helpful. This person may know nothing about [Company] or may have inherited it. Meet them where they are. |
| **After Sending** | Log in Chatter. Follow up with a calendar invite for the introduction call. |

| **TEMPLATE 3.11 — New Stakeholder Welcome** | |
| --- | --- |
| **To** | New Stakeholder (new hire / replacement contact / new decision maker) |
| **Subject** | Welcome — I'd love to connect and introduce myself |
| **Body** | Hi [Name],  Welcome to [Company] — or congratulations on your new role!  I wanted to reach out and introduce myself. I'm [CSM Name], your Customer Success Manager at [Company]. I work closely with your team to make sure you're getting the most out of the platform and that [Company] continues to support your goals as they evolve.  Your team has accomplished a lot with [Company] — [one specific highlight, e.g., 'your admissions team has been consistently tracking referral sources and your BD pipeline visibility has grown significantly since implementation'].  I'd love to share more of that context and learn about your priorities as you settle into your role.Would you have 30 minutes in the next week or two for a brief introduction call? [Calendar link or suggested times]  Looking forward to meeting you!  [CSM Name]Customer Success Manager, [Company] |
| **Tips** | *Personalize the value highlight — use their actual data. If you don't know them yet, keep it brief and focus on the offer to connect. Do not send a product tour or a list of features — lead with the relationship.* |

**TEMPLATE 3.12 — LEADERSHIP TURNOVER OUTREACH**

**⚠** Sent when a senior leader or executive changes at the customer organization. Higher stakes than a standard new stakeholder welcome — this person may question the entire [Company] investment. Lead with value, move fast, and get a call on the calendar.

| **When to Send** | Within 48 hours of learning about a leadership change. Do not wait to be introduced — be proactive. |
| --- | --- |
| **Sent By** | CSM (coordinate with Anna if the new leader is C-suite) |
| **Tone** | Professional, confident, and value-led. Brief — executives do not read long emails. One specific win, one clear ask. |
| **After Sending** | Log in Chatter. Follow up with a calendar invite. If no response within 5 business days, escalate to Anna for executive-to-executive outreach. |

| **TEMPLATE 3.12A — Leadership Turnover Outreach — Initial** | |
| --- | --- |
| **To** | New Leader / Executive |
| **Subject** | Introducing myself — and what your team has built with [Company] |
| **Body** | Hi [Name], Congratulations on your new role at [Company]!  I'm [CSM Name], your Customer Success Manager at [Company]. I work with your team to make sure the CRM is driving real value for your admissions and BD operations.  Your organization has accomplished some great things with [Company] — [one specific, data-backed highlight, e.g., 'your admissions team has tracked over 400 referral source interactions this quarter and your BD pipeline is fully managed in the platform']. I'd love to share more of that story and understand your priorities as you step into this role.Would you have 20-30 minutes in the next week or two?  [Calendar link]  Looking forward to meeting you.  [CSM Name]Customer Success Manager, [Company] |
| **Tips** | *Keep it short — two paragraphs max. One specific win with data. One clear ask (a call). Do not pitch features. Do not attach anything. If you know anything about their background or priorities, weave it in subtly.* |

| **TEMPLATE 3.12B — Leadership Turnover Outreach — No Response Follow-Up** | |
| --- | --- |
| **To** | New Leader / Executive |
| **Subject** | Following up — happy to connect at your convenience |
| **Body** | Hi [Name],I wanted to follow up on my note from last week in case it got lost in the transition.I'm here whenever it works for you — even a brief 15-minute call would be a great start. I can share what your team has accomplished with [Company] and make sure you have everything you need from us going forward.[Calendar link]Take care,[CSM Name]Customer Success Manager, [Company] |
| **Tips** | *Send 5 business days after 3.12A with no response. Very short. Low pressure. If still no response after this — escalate to Anna for an executive-to-executive outreach from [Company] leadership.* |
