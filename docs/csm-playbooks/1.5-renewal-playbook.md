**SECTION 1 — OVERVIEW**

| **Trigger** | Renewal is 120 days out. CSM begins internal prep immediately — no customer outreach yet. |
| --- | --- |
| **Goal** | By the time the 60-day notice goes out, there are no surprises. Forecast category is set with confidence, health score is current, economic buyer is confirmed, seat count is clean, and the CSM knows exactly where the account stands. |
| **Ideal State** | At 4 months out, the CSM has a strong read on the renewal forecast, has reviewed health, has some indication from the customer about renewal intent based on their lead-up meetings, and has a clean seat and product audit. |
| **Stakeholders** | CSM, Anna (approvals + escalations), Finance (billing confirmation), Customer POC, Economic Buyer (signer) |
| **Escalation Path** | CSM → Anna → Sarah |
| **Key Rule** | All notes live in Chatter (SFDC) — NOT Slack. Every renewal email send, conversation, and negotiation must be logged. |

**SECTION 2 — 120-DAY INTERNAL PREP CHECKLIST**

**⚠** Complete all items below before initiating any customer renewal conversation. If data is missing or wrong at this stage, fix it now — not during the renewal conversation.

| **Prep Item** | **What to Do** | **Where in SFDC** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Set Renewal Forecast Category** | Assign Positive Outlook / Neutral / At Risk / Intent to Churn based on current health and engagement. This should not be a guess — if you are unsure, it is Neutral until you know more. | *Renewal Object → Forecast picklist* |
| **Review Health Score — all 5 pillars** | Pull the Account Health Overview report filtered to your book. Fix any stale data — blank Sentiment, missing Meeting Cadence, no EB interaction logged. Do not enter a renewal conversation with a wrong health score. | *SFDC: Account Health Overview Report* |
| **Confirm Economic Buyer** | Who is signing the paperwork? Who gives the final thumbs up? These are not always the same person. Verify the Economic Buyer field in SFDC has the correct contact. If it is blank, find out and fill it in before renewal conversations start. | *Account → Economic Buyer field (SFDC contact)* |
| **Seat Audit — CRM** | Check purchased seats vs. provisioned seats vs. actively used seats. Identify any self-serve seat additions that were not logged as Expansion Opps. Fix internally first — do not raise with the customer yet. Fold any discrepancy into the renewal conversation. | *Account → Seat fields SFDC: Expansion Opp check* |
| **Product Audit — iQ / iCampaign / iVerify** | Review purchased products vs. active usage where data is available. Note any significant gaps between what was purchased and what is being used — these are risks and opportunities. iQ usage baselines are still being established — document what you can see today. | *Account → Product Usage Score SFDC: Product Usage fields* |
| **Check for Open Risk Flags** | Any Active or Recovering risk flags must be addressed before renewal conversations begin. A renewal entering with an unresolved Active flag should be classified At Risk minimum. | *Risk\_Flag\_\_c → Status = Active / Recovering* |
| **Review Expansion Opportunities** | Are there upsell conversations worth having before or at renewal? Additional seats, product additions, or tier upgrades. Log any identified opps in SFDC. | *SFDC → Expansion Opportunity* |
| **Confirm Renewal Date and Billing Contact** | Verify the renewal date is correct. Confirm who the billing contact is and who should receive the new agreement. Update SFDC if anything has changed. | *Account → Renewal Date Renewal Object → Billing Contact* |

**SECTION 3 — RENEWAL PROCESS STEPS**

| **#** | **Step** | **Action** | **Owner** | **Timing / Resources** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **1** | **120-Day Internal Prep** | At 120 days out, the CSM begins internal renewal prep. No customer outreach yet. See the 120-Day Prep Checklist in Section 2 for the full list. Key items: set forecast category, review health score, confirm economic buyer, run seat audit, check for risk flags and expansion opps. | CSM | 120 days before renewal SFDC: Account Health Overview Report SFDC: Renewal Object → Forecast See Section 2 checklist |
| **2** | **Move to Internal Discovery in SFDC** | Update the Renewal Opportunity stage to Internal Discovery. This is the default starting stage. Confirm renewal date, ARR, seat count, and billing contact are all accurate in SFDC before any customer conversation begins. | CSM | 120–90 days before renewal Renewal Object → Stage = Internal Discovery |
| **3** | **Initiate Renewal Conversation with Customer** | Begin the renewal conversation in your normal call cadence — QBR, regular check-in, or scheduled renewal call. Do not make it feel transactional. Gauge sentiment, confirm they know renewal is coming, and get a read on where they stand. Update SFDC stage to Renewal Initiated with Customer. Update forecast category if anything has changed. | CSM | 90–60 days before renewal Renewal Object → Stage = Renewal Initiated with Customer Chatter — log conversation notes |
| **4** | **Send 60-Day Renewal Notice** | Send the 60-Day Renewal Notice email using the approved template. Personalize with at least 2–3 specific value highlights before sending. Confirm: who receives the new agreement, and which billing path they prefer (monthly with 7% adjustment or annual to maintain current rate). Update SFDC stage to 60 Day Notice. Log send in Chatter. | CSM | 60 days before renewal Comm Template 3.5 — 60-Day Notice Renewal Object → Stage = 60 Day Notice Chatter — log send |
| **5** | **Send 45-Day Reminder (if no response)** | If no response to the 60-day notice, send the 45-Day Reminder. Re-emphasize value. Reiterate the annual billing offer. Remind customer that any plan changes (seat reductions, adjustments) must be made no later than 30 days before renewal. If still no response after this email, update forecast to Neutral minimum. Update SFDC stage to 45 Day Notice. Log in Chatter. | CSM | 45 days before renewal Comm Template 3.6 — 45-Day Notice Renewal Object → Stage = 45 Day Notice Chatter — log send |
| **6** | **Send 30-Day Final Notice** | Send the 30-Day Final Notice. This is the last chance for plan changes. Subscription auto-renews at updated terms if no action is taken. If there is still no response after three emails, escalate to Anna immediately. Do not let an unresponsive account auto-renew without a conversation. Update SFDC stage to 30 Day Notice. Log in Chatter. | CSM | 30 days before renewal Comm Template 3.7 — 30-Day Notice Renewal Object → Stage = 30 Day Notice Chatter — log send Escalate to Anna if no response |
| **7** | **Renewal Paperwork** | Quote is built in SFDC. Sarah approves in SFDC. DocuSign is sent to the customer from SFDC. Update SFDC stage to Renewal Paperwork. | CSM + Sarah | At any point — ideally 30+ days out SFDC → Quote → DocuSign Renewal Object → Stage = Renewal Paperwork |
| **8** | **Negotiation (if applicable)** | See Negotiation Guardrails in Section 4. The standard offer: switch to annual billing → 7% increase is waived. This is the only trade CSMs can make independently. Expansion (seats or products): 7% can also be waived. Anything beyond this requires Sarah’s approval. | CSM (Sarah if escalated) | During paperwork phase See Section 4 — Negotiation Guardrails Chatter — document all negotiation notes |
| **9** | **DocuSign Signed — Both Parties** | Both Sarah and the customer have signed. Update SFDC stage to DocuSign Sent → then Closed Won once Finance confirms. Finance reviews and confirms billing is correct. Opportunity moves to Closed Won in SFDC. Slack channel is pinged — team celebrates. 🎉 CSM returns to standard engagement cadence. | Sarah + Finance + CSM | Ideally 10+ days before renewal SFDC → Opportunity → Closed Won Slack — renewal celebration channel Chatter — log confirmation |

**SECTION 4 — RENEWAL SCENARIO GUIDE**

**ℹ** Use this guide to calibrate your approach based on account health and forecast category. The right renewal motion for a green account is completely different from a red one.

| **Scenario** | **Health / Forecast** | **Approach** | **On Annual Billing?** | **Escalate?** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Healthy Renewal** | Green / Positive Outlook | Go for it. Push for annual billing, land the 7% uplift, and try to get it signed early. This is a win — close it fast. | If not on annual: offer the trade — switch to annual and the 7% increase goes away. This is the standard offer. | No — CSM owns this end to end. |
| **Neutral Renewal** | Yellow / Neutral | Tread lightly. Test the waters on annual billing and the 7% — don't lead with it. A flat renewal at current terms is always acceptable. If you don't know where they stand, just ask directly. Do not let it linger. | Offer annual billing as an option but do not push it. If they decline, take the flat monthly renewal. Do not push the 7% increase without escalating first. | Loop Anna in if ambiguous or if renewal is stalling. |
| **At-Risk Renewal** | Red / At Risk | A Risk Flag must be filed. A documented Save Plan must exist on the Renewal Opp in SFDC. Ask for help — this is not a solo mission. Detail the plan and bring Anna in. | Annual billing conversation is off the table until the risk is resolved. Focus on securing any renewal first. | **Yes — Anna/Sarah must be involved. Escalate immediately.** |

**SECTION 5 — NEGOTIATION GUARDRAILS**

**★** The standard trade: annual billing = no 7% increase. This is the only trade CSMs can make independently. Everything else requires Anna's approval.

| **Situation** | **CSM Can Offer** | **Requires Escalation** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Customer on monthly billing** | Waive the 7% increase in exchange for switching to annual billing. This is the standard trade — CSM can offer this independently. | No escalation needed. CSM owns this offer. |
| **Customer expanding (adding seats or products)** | Waive the 7% increase. Expansion is a win — don't penalize growth with a rate increase. | No escalation needed. CSM can waive the 7% independently when expansion is confirmed. |
| **Customer wants to stay monthly AND avoid the 7% increase** | CSM cannot offer both. Monthly billing without the 7% increase is not within CSM authority. | Escalate to Sarah/Anna. Do not agree to this without approval. |
| **Customer wants to reduce seats or downgrade** | CSM should not agree to any seat reduction or plan downgrade without escalation. | Escalate to Sarah/Anna immediately. Document in Chatter. |
| **Customer wants to extend terms or negotiate custom pricing** | Outside CSM authority entirely. | Escalate to Anna → Sarah as needed. |

**SECTION 7 — CLOSED LOST PROCESS**

**✕** Closed Lost requires a Churn Report — no exceptions. This data is critical for leadership reporting and understanding why we lose customers.

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **1** | **Mark Opportunity Closed Lost in SFDC** | Update the Renewal Opportunity to Closed Lost in SFDC. Select the correct Closed Lost Reason from the picklist — this data feeds churn analysis and is important for leadership reporting. | CSM | When renewal is confirmed lost SFDC → Renewal Opportunity → Closed Lost Closed Lost Reason picklist |
| **2** | **Complete the Churn Report** | Fill out the Churn Report Google Form completely and accurately. This is not optional — every Closed Lost renewal requires a Churn Report. The Churn Report captures: customer details, churn reason, timeline, what was tried, and what could have been done differently. | CSM | See [2.9 | Churn Report SOP](https://docs.google.com/document/d/12dj6q9ifcGvsf3AaXUaUCFXbNmxhZoyQ/edit) |
| **3** | **Log Full Context in Chatter** | Log a comprehensive note in Chatter covering the full renewal story — what happened, when, what was tried, what the customer said, and any lessons learned. This is the record leadership will reference. | CSM | Within 24 hours of Closed Lost Account → Chatter feed |
