**SECTION 1 — OVERVIEW**

| **Trigger** | The At-Risk Save Plan is not working, the customer has expressed explicit churn intent, or Health Score is critically low with no recovery in sight. |
| --- | --- |
| **Difference from At-Risk Playbook** | The At-Risk Playbook (1.6) is proactive intervention. The Churn Save Playbook is the emergency response when the At-Risk plan has failed or time has run out. By the time this playbook is triggered, Anna must already be involved. |
| **Goal** | Make one final, genuine, well-coordinated effort to save the account — or transition out professionally if the customer's decision is final. |
| **Key Principle** | A Churn Save is not about being desperate. It is about making sure the customer has every opportunity to stay and that [Company] has done everything reasonable to address their concerns. If they still choose to leave, accept it with professionalism. |
| **Escalation Path** | CSM → Anna → Sarah |
| **Key Rule** | All notes in Chatter — not Slack. Churn Retrospective must be completed within 24 hours of Closed Lost. |

**✕** If you are at this playbook and Anna is not already involved — stop. Escalate to Anna before taking any further action. A Churn Save without management involvement is not a Churn Save.

| **3** | **Make the Personal Outreach Call** | Before sending any email, call the customer directly. Not a scheduled call — a direct, personal call from the CSM.Be honest: "I want to make sure I understand what is not working and see if there is anything we can do differently."Listen more than you talk.
