**SECTION 1 — OVERVIEW**

| **Trigger** | A senior leader or executive at the customer organization — CEO, COO, Director of Admissions, Director of BD, or any other key decision maker — has changed. |
| --- | --- |
| **Difference from Champion Loss** | Champion Loss (1.9) is about the day-to-day internal advocate. Leadership Turnover is about the executive or senior leader — the person who signs the contract, approves the budget, or sets the technology direction. The risk profile is higher. |
| **Goal** | Re-establish the executive relationship before the new leader questions the [Company] investment, brings in their preferred technology, or makes a renewal decision without adequate context. |
| **Key Risk** | New leaders often bring the technology they know. If you do not get in front of them quickly and demonstrate value with their data, they will form their own opinion — and it may not be favorable. |
| **Escalation Path** | CSM → Anna → Sarah |
| **Key Rule** | All notes in Chatter — not Slack. File Leadership Turnover risk flag immediately. |

**SECTION 2 — PROCESS STEPS**

**✕** Speed matters more in Leadership Turnover than almost any other scenario. The new leader is forming their opinion of [Company] before you know they exist. Move fast.
