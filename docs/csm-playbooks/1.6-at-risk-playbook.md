**SECTION 1 — OVERVIEW**

| **Trigger** | Account Health Score drops below 65, an Active Risk Flag is filed, or CSM identifies a risk signal before it shows in the data. |
| --- | --- |
| **Goal** | Intervene early, document a specific Save Plan, execute with discipline, and either recover the account or escalate in time to mount a real save effort. |
| **Key Principle** | At-Risk accounts are not lost accounts. Most churn is preventable if the CSM acts early enough and has a real plan. A vague Save Plan is not a plan — it is a document that makes leadership feel better while the account churns. |
| **Save Plan** | Three components: (1) CSM Save Plan field on the Renewal Opp in SFDC — document the plan here. (2) #customername\_internal Slack channel — internal save strategy conversation. (3) Chatter + Active Risk Flags — the context that should already exist. |

**SECTION 2 — AT-RISK SIGNALS BY HEALTH SCORE PILLAR**

**ℹ** An account can be at risk on one pillar while appearing healthy on others. Always review all 5 pillars when a score drops — not just the overall number.

| **Pillar** | **At-Risk Signal** | **CSM Action** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Risk Flag Score (0.10)** | Active Risk Flag exists on account | Address the root cause. Move to Recovering as progress is made. See Risk Flag SOP (2.3). |
| **Customer Engagement (low)** | Meeting Cadence below Monthly, Sentiment Negative or Unknown, EB Interaction > 90 days | Re-establish contact. Update Sentiment field. Log EB interaction. Escalate to Anna for Leadership Alignment if exec is unreachable. |
| **Product Usage (low)** | 1-day or 7-day usage ratio below 0.50. Usage declining over 6-month window | Identify the blocker — training gap, feature gap, or leadership not enforcing adoption. Schedule enablement session. File Customer Adoption risk flag. |
| **Financial Score (low)** | Outstanding balance > 10% of ARR | Loop in Finance/Collections immediately. Do not try to manage billing issues without Finance. File Financial Issue risk flag. |
| **Support Score (low)** | High volume of open or recurring tickets | Review ticket queue. Identify patterns. Escalate stale tickets. File Support Tickets risk flag if pattern is systemic. |

**SECTION 4 — SAVE PLAN GUIDE**

**⚠** The CSM Save Plan field on the Renewal Opp is the single source of truth for the save effort. Write it as if you are briefing Sarah and Marcel on what is happening and what you are doing about it.

| **Save Plan Component** | **What to Include** |
| --- | --- |
| **Root Cause** | What is actually driving the risk? Not the symptom — the root cause. 'Low adoption' is a symptom. 'Director of Admissions refuses to enforce platform use and the CSM has no exec access' is a root cause. |
| **What Has Been Tried** | List every action taken so far and what the result was. This prevents duplicating efforts and shows leadership you have already been working the problem. |
| **Specific Actions** | List each planned action with an owner and a target date. Vague actions are not actions. 'Improve engagement' is not an action. 'Schedule bi-weekly calls with Dir of Admissions through May — CSM owns, first call by March 25' is an action. |
| **Definition of Success** | What does recovered look like? A specific health score target, a renewed contract, a confirmed exec sponsor? Make it measurable. |
| **Fallback Plan** | If the save plan does not work, what happens next? Escalate to Sarah? Trigger Churn Save playbook? Accept the loss and focus on offboarding cleanly? |
