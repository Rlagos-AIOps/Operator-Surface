**SECTION 1 — OVERVIEW**

**⚠** Used when an account is flagged At-Risk, engagement has dropped significantly, or the CSM needs to re-establish contact about account health. Always try to call first — this email follows the call or is sent if the call is not answered.

| **When to Send** | When an account is flagged At-Risk, engagement has dropped significantly, or the CSM needs to re-open a conversation about account health. Always try calling first — use this email as a follow-up if the call is not answered, or to confirm what was discussed. |
| --- | --- |
| **Sent By** | CSM |
| **Tone** | Caring and direct — not desperate or accusatory. The CSM noticed something and wants to understand. The door is open. |
| **Two Variants** | 3.8A — Re-engagement when the customer has gone quiet or health signals have dropped. 3.8B — Follow-up after a difficult conversation, committing to specific actions. |
| **After Sending** | Log the send in Chatter. Update Customer Sentiment field. If no response within 5 business days, escalate to Anna. |
| **Difference from 3.9** | 3.8 At-Risk Outreach is for when you know there is a problem — health score dropped, risk flag filed, or a difficult call has happened. 3.9 Re-engagement is for when the customer has simply gone quiet with no known issue. |

**SECTION 2 — EMAIL TEMPLATES**

| **TEMPLATE 3.8A — At-Risk Outreach — Re-engagement** | |
| --- | --- |
| **To** | Customer POC / Primary Contact |
| **Subject** | Checking in — I want to make sure we're set up for success |
| **Body** | Hi [Customer Name],  I wanted to reach out personally — I've noticed [specific observation: e.g., 'we haven't connected in a while' / 'usage across your team has dropped over the last few weeks' / 'your team hasn't had a chance to use some of the features we set up together'], and I want to make sure everything is going well on your end.  My goal is always to make sure [Company] is genuinely working for your team — not just sitting in the background. If something isn't clicking, I'd much rather know now so we can address it together. Would you have 20 minutes this week for a quick call?  I'd love to hear how things are going and make sure we're focused on what matters most to you right now. [Calendar link or suggested times]  Looking forward to connecting!  [CSM Name] Customer Success Manager, [Company] |
| **Tips** | *Be specific about what you noticed — 'usage has dropped' is more credible than a generic check-in. Do not send this without first attempting a phone call. Log send in Chatter and update Customer Sentiment.* |

| **TEMPLATE 3.8B — At-Risk Outreach — After Difficult Conversation** | |
| --- | --- |
| **To** | Customer POC / Economic Buyer |
| **Subject** | Following up on our conversation — here is what we are going to do |
| **Body** | Hi [Customer Name],  Thank you for being so candid with me [today / earlier this week]. I really appreciate your honesty — it is exactly the kind of feedback that helps us make [Company] work better for your team.  Here is what I heard from you:  - [Summary of concern 1]  - [Summary of concern 2]  And here is what I am committing to:  - [Specific action 1 — owner — timeline]  - [Specific action 2 — owner — timeline]  I want to make sure you feel the difference, not just hear about it. Let us plan to reconnect on [date] to review progress. Thank you again for giving us the opportunity to make this right.  [CSM Name] Customer Success Manager, [Company] |
| **Tips** | *Use this after a difficult call where concerns were raised. Be specific about what you heard and what you are committing to — vague commitments erode trust. Log in Chatter. Update Customer Sentiment. Document commitments in SFDC Save Plan field if applicable.* |
