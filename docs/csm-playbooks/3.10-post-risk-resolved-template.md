**SECTION 1 — OVERVIEW**

**✓** Sent after a Risk Flag has been moved to Resolved AND the customer has confirmed the issue is gone. Closes the loop professionally. Rebuilds confidence. Do not send before the customer confirms — premature resolution emails erode trust if the issue resurfaces.

| **When to Send** | Risk Flag is Resolved in SFDC AND customer has confirmed the issue is gone. Not before. |
| --- | --- |
| **Sent By** | CSM |
| **Tone** | Confident, warm, and forward-looking. Acknowledge what happened briefly — don't dwell. Focus on what was done and where you go from here. |
| **After Sending** | Update Customer Sentiment to Positive (if warranted). Log send in Chatter. This is also a good moment to look for expansion signals — a customer who just had a problem resolved and feels well-served is often more open to a deeper conversation. |
| **Two Variants** | 3.10A for standard resolved issues. 3.10B for significant issues where leadership was involved. |

**SECTION 2 — EMAIL TEMPLATES**

| **TEMPLATE 3.10A — Post-Risk-Flag-Resolved — Standard** | |
| --- | --- |
| **To** | Customer POC + any stakeholders involved in the issue |
| **Subject** | Great news — [issue] is resolved |
| **Body** | Hi [Customer Name],  I wanted to follow up and officially confirm that [brief description of issue — e.g., 'the EHR integration sync issue'] has been fully resolved.  Here is a quick summary of what happened and what we did:  - [What the issue was — one sentence]  - [What was done to fix it — one sentence]  - [How we confirmed it is working — one sentence]  Thank you for your patience while we worked through this — I know it caused some disruption for your team and I appreciate how you handled it. From here, we are back to our normal cadence. Is there anything else on your mind, or anything I can help with?  [CSM Name] Customer Success Manager, [Company] |
| **Tips** | *Be specific about what was fixed and how it was confirmed. Do not be vague — 'the issue has been resolved' with no explanation sounds like a form letter. Use the customer's own words from earlier conversations if possible.* |

| **TEMPLATE 3.10B — Post-Risk-Flag-Resolved — Significant Issue, Leadership Involved** | |
| --- | --- |
| **To** | Customer POC + Economic Buyer |
| **Subject** | Following up — and thank you for working through this with us |
| **Body** | Hi [Customer Name],  I wanted to take a moment to personally follow up now that [brief description of issue] has been fully resolved.  I know this was frustrating — it affected your team's workflows and required more of your time and attention than it should have. I want you to know that we took it seriously at every level. Here is what we did:  - [Action 1]  - [Action 2]  - [How we confirmed resolution]  We have also [any process change or safeguard put in place to prevent recurrence, if applicable].  Thank you for your patience and for giving us the opportunity to make it right. That kind of partnership is exactly what makes these relationships work long term. Looking forward to our next conversation.  [CSM Name] Customer Success Manager, [Company] |
| **Tips** | *Use this variant when the issue was serious enough to involve Anna or Sarah, or when it caused significant disruption. Acknowledge the impact directly — minimizing it will feel dismissive.* |
